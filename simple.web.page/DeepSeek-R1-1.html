What Are Aurora Borealis?

The Northern Lights are a natural light display in Earth's sky, predominantly seen in high-latitude regions around the Arctic. These mesmerizing curtains of light occur when charged particles from the sun collide with gases in our atmosphere.

Why They Matter

Beyond their breathtaking beauty, auroras help scientists study space weather and understand Earth's magnetic field. Many cultures have rich mythology surrounding the lights, from Viking beliefs to Indigenous traditions.
