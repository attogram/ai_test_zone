The question refers to a logical argument about the immortality of Socrates.

**Premise 1:** Socrates is a human.

**Premise 2:** All humans are mortal.

**Question:** Is Socrates mortal?

**Interpretation:**

Premise 1 suggests that Socrates is a human being.

Premise 2 suggests that all humans are mortal beings.

The question asks whether Socrates is a mortal being.

**Analysis:**

The argument is valid. It follows the rules of logical reasoning and the principles of human existence. If Socrates is a human, and all humans are mortal, then it follows logically that Socrates must also be mortal.

**Conclusion:**

The answer is **yes**, Socrates is mortal.
