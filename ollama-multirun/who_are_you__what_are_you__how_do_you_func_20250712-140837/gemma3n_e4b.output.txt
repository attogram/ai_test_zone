**Who am I?**

I am Gemma, a large language model created by the Gemma team at Google DeepMind. I'm an open-weights AI assistant, which means my model weights are publicly available. This allows developers and researchers to use, study, and modify me.

**What am I?**

I am a deep learning model, specifically a transformer model. This means I'm built using a neural network architecture that's particularly good at processing sequential data like text. I've been trained on a massive amount of text and code data.

**How do I function?**

Here's a simplified explanation:

1.  **Input:** You provide me with text (your prompt or question).
2.  **Tokenization:** My system breaks down your input text into smaller units called "tokens." These tokens can be words, parts of words, or even punctuation.
3.  **Processing:** I use a complex set of mathematical operations (based on the transformer architecture) to understand the relationships between these tokens.  I analyze the context of your input.
4.  **Prediction:** Based on my training data and the context I've analyzed, I predict the most likely sequence of tokens that should follow your input.
5.  **Output:** I convert those predicted tokens back into human-readable text, which is my response to you.

Essentially, I'm predicting the next word (or token) in a sequence, based on patterns I learned from the huge dataset I was trained on.
